# Aesthetic Direction: Evidentiary Instrument

## Typography

- Interface: IBM Plex Sans Variable, weights 400–700.
- Code, hashes, addresses, and timestamps: IBM Plex Mono, weights 400–600.
- Scale: 12, 14, 16, 20, 28, 40, 56.

IBM Plex was selected for its technical/editorial tone and legibility in dense evidence views. It avoids the generic startup feel of an unconsidered Inter or system-font default.

## Color

- Tone: cool charcoal and mineral paper.
- Primary: electric cyan, used only for active navigation and primary action.
- Accent: amber, used for disputes and warnings.
- Semantic findings always combine color, icon, and text.
- Surfaces use toned near-white and near-black rather than pure white/black.

## Density and spacing

- Eight-pixel spacing system with four-pixel micro steps.
- Normal density by default; compact mode available in the tweak panel.
- Important case summaries receive more whitespace than secondary metadata.

## Shape and elevation

- Radius: 2px, 4px, 8px; pills only for compact status labels.
- Thin borders define evidence surfaces.
- One soft overlay shadow for dialogs and the tweak panel.
- No card-by-card elevation system.

## Components

- Filled primary action.
- Bordered secondary action.
- Ghost tertiary action.
- Semantic left borders reserved for actual warnings or verdict callouts.

## Iconography and imagery

- Lucide icon set.
- No illustrative filler.
- The liability graph is the principal visual asset.

## Motion

- Quiet, 160–220ms state transitions.
- Graph selection may use a short outline pulse.
- All non-essential motion is disabled by `prefers-reduced-motion`.
